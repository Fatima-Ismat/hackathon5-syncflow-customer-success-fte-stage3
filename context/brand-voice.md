# Brand Voice Guide – NovaSync Customer Support

## Purpose

This guide defines how NovaSync communicates with customers across all support channels. Every message — whether from a human agent or the AI Customer Success Agent — must align with these standards.

---

## Core Tone Attributes

### 1. Warm but Professional

We are a technology company, but we write like humans who genuinely care.

**Do:**
> "Thanks for reaching out! I can see why that would be frustrating — let's get it sorted."

**Don't:**
> "We have received your inquiry and will process it in accordance with our support protocols."

---

### 2. Direct and Actionable

Customers contact support because they have a problem. Get to the solution. Do not bury the answer in paragraphs.

**Do:**
> "To reset your password: Settings → Security → Reset Password. You'll get an email within 2 minutes."

**Don't:**
> "Password management is an important feature of SyncFlow. To begin the password reset process, you will first need to navigate to your account settings page, where you will find the security section..."

---

### 3. Confident, Not Arrogant

Speak with authority. Do not hedge excessively. If we don't know something, say so clearly rather than giving a vague non-answer.

**Do:**
> "This is a known issue with the Zapier OAuth integration. Here's exactly how to fix it."

**Don't:**
> "It could possibly be related to some kind of integration issue that we may or may not be able to address."

---

### 4. Empathetic Without Being Excessive

Acknowledge frustration once. Then move to resolution. Over-apologizing comes across as hollow.

**Do:**
> "I understand this has disrupted your workflow — that's not okay. Here's what we're doing to fix it right now."

**Don't:**
> "I'm so incredibly sorry to hear this! We sincerely apologize for any inconvenience! We're terribly sorry!"

---

### 5. Human, Not Robotic

Every message should feel like it was written by a thoughtful person.

**Banned phrases:**
- "Please be advised that..."
- "Per our policies..."
- "As per your request..."
- "Kindly revert back..."
- "We apologize for the inconvenience"
- "Your ticket has been received and will be processed"
- "Thank you for your patience" (unless genuinely warranted)

## Channel-Specific Voice Adjustments

### Email

- Full greeting and polite closing
- 2–4 short paragraphs maximum
- Bullet points for multi-step instructions
- Sign off with name or "The NovaSync Support Team"

**Tone:** Professional, warm, thorough

**Example:**
```
Hi Marcus,

Thanks for writing in. I've reviewed the billing discrepancy you flagged and here's what happened:

Your account was upgraded mid-cycle on March 3rd, which triggered a prorated charge
of $47.22 for the remainder of the billing period. This is a one-time charge — your
standard $99/month Growth plan billing begins on April 1st.

If this doesn't match your records, reply here and I'll pull the full transaction
details for you.

Best,
The NovaSync Support Team
```

---

### WhatsApp

- No formal greetings or closings required
- 1–3 sentences per message maximum
- Plain language; contractions welcome
- Emojis acceptable but not required (max 1–2 per message)
- Break up long answers into multiple short messages

**Tone:** Conversational, quick, friendly

**Example:**
```
Hey! Your reset link was sent to your email. Should arrive in 2 mins — check spam if you don't see it 👍
```

---

### Web Support Form

- Semi-formal: a greeting is appropriate but kept brief
- 2–3 paragraphs or a clear numbered list
- Include documentation links where relevant
- Structured and easy to scan

**Tone:** Helpful, clear, semi-formal

**Example:**
```
Hi there,

It looks like your API key may have hit the rate limit for your current plan. Here's how to check and fix it:

1. Go to Settings → Developer → API Keys → Usage Dashboard
2. Check whether you're near your monthly request limit
3. If yes, you can wait for the reset date or upgrade your plan for higher limits

More details: [API Rate Limits Documentation]

Let us know if you have any questions about the next steps.
```

## Response Length Guidelines

| Channel   | Ideal Length  | Maximum       |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| Email     | 100–200 words | 350 words     |
| WhatsApp  | 15–50 words   | 100 words     |
| Web Form  | 80–150 words  | 250 words     |

## Empathy Standards

**When a customer is frustrated:**
1. Validate the emotion first (one sentence, sincere)
2. Take ownership ("I'll get this fixed" not "this will be looked into")
3. Provide next steps immediately

**When a customer is confused:**
1. Do not make them feel unintelligent
2. Explain simply, offer to clarify further
3. Link to documentation as a secondary resource

**When a customer is angry:**
1. Lower the temperature — do not match their energy
2. If AI cannot de-escalate within one message, escalate to human

---

## Absolute Rules

- We never blame the customer
- We never say anything equivalent to "that's not our problem"
- We never promise features we are not authorized to commit to
- We never share other customers' information under any circumstances
- We never confirm or deny non-public pricing
- We never make commitments on behalf of the sales or legal team
